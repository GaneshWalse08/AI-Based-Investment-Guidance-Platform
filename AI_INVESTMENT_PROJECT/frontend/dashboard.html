📖 Guide to Fixed Deposits (FDs)

A Fixed Deposit allows you to deposit a lump sum of money with a bank for a fixed duration, earning a guaranteed rate of interest. It is entirely unaffected by stock market crashes. However, the biggest hidden risk to an FD is Inflation. If your bank gives you 7% interest, but daily goods get 6% more expensive, your "Real Return" is only 1%. 🏗️ Module 1: How the Stock Market Works

🏦

The Core Concept

The stock market is simply a digital marketplace. Instead of buying vegetables or clothes, buyers and sellers come together to trade ownership stakes (called shares) in real-world businesses.

🍋 The Lemonade Stand Analogy

1

The Idea & Funding

You want to open a lemonade stand, which costs ₹1,000 to start. You only have ₹500. You ask a friend to invest the remaining ₹500. You issue 10 total shares of your business. You take 5, your friend gets 5.

2

Dividends (Profit Sharing)

At the end of the month, the stand makes ₹200 in pure profit. Because your friend owns 50% of the shares, they are entitled to ₹100. When companies pay out profits to shareholders, this is called a Dividend.

3

Capital Appreciation

Your stand becomes famous! Someone offers to buy the whole business for ₹10,000. Your friend's original ₹500 investment (their 5 shares) is now worth ₹5,000. This increase in the value of the stock is Capital Appreciation.

🌱 Investing (Wealth Creation)

  • ✔️ Focus: Buying quality businesses and holding them for years.
  • ✔️ Strategy: Fundamental analysis (revenue, ESG score, debt).
  • ✔️ Goal: Gradual wealth generation through compound interest.
  • ✔️ Stress Level: Low. You don't need to check prices every day.

⚡ Trading (Speculation)

  • ⚠️ Focus: Buying and selling rapidly (often same-day).
  • ⚠️ Strategy: Technical analysis (charts, momentum, patterns).
  • ⚠️ Goal: Quick profits based on market psychology.
  • ⚠️ Stress Level: High. Requires constant monitoring of screens.

📖 Essential Market Glossary

The vocabulary you need to navigate financial reports and news.

🏢 Market Cap

The total value of a company. Calculated by multiplying the current share price by the total number of outstanding shares.

🐂 Bull Market

A financial market where prices are rising or are expected to rise. General optimism and buying is high.

🐻 Bear Market

A market condition where prices are falling, usually by 20% or more from recent highs. Pessimism drives selling.

⚖️ P/E Ratio

Price-to-Earnings. A valuation metric comparing a company's current share price to its per-share earnings.

📈 Volatility

A measure of price swings. High volatility means the price moves wildly; low volatility means it is stable.

🌍 ESG Score

Evaluating Environmental, Social, and Governance factors alongside financial data to determine sustainability.
